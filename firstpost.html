Gun Violence in Schools

Recent events show gun violence in schools has proven to be a major issue, but how do we change that?

Posted by Anna Milnes on April 25, 2023

Following recent gun violence events in schools, it has become crucial the U.S. needs to change something.

Accordion to JHU Hub’s gun violence center, “permits to purchase, child access protection laws, and extreme risk protection orders all reduce the likelihood of firearm deaths.”

The U.S. has easier access to military-grade weaponry, making it easier for traumatic events like school shootings to happen. It is clear not only the U.S. has problems with citizens with violent thoughts, but easy access to guns makes it significantly more like those people will act on those thoughts.

Law enforcement on school grounds

One thing the U.S. has done to help prevent school shootings includes placing more law enforcement officers on school grounds. This has proven to not be successful in stopping violence and actually negatively affects students.

Studies have shown having law enforcement officers on school grounds lowers student test scores, and on college campuses, it has drastically lower attendance in classes. This is not the best way to go about stopping gun violence, instead, we should be focusing on increasing gun control regulations.

Gun violence in the U.S. compared to other countries

YouTube, 22 Feb. 2016, https://youtu.be/bX4qUsgHa4Y. Accessed 26 Apr. 2023.

Gun violence is a problem the United States struggles with more than any other country. The number of deaths in the united states from aids, illegal drug overdoses, and terrorism combined is less than the number of gun deaths. Because of this, something needs to change in how we regulate buying firearms.

The more guns in the U.S. the more likely someone will get their hands on one, who shouldn't be allowed to. there are over three hundred million guns currently in the U.S. so how do we know all of those will be used properly, and not for violence? The answer is we don't.

On average ninety-two people are killed daily by guns, this includes school shootings, suicide, gang violence, and more. It's time we put a stop to this violence in our country, enough is enough. To start we need to implement better gun control.
